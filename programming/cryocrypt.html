CryoCrypt

I am a third year master's student at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln studying Computer Science. I also have a Bachelor's of Science in Computer Science from Kansas State University, where I focused my studies on cyber security and video game development. I am currently the president of the UNL Game Development Club and studying educational game development/gamification as my master's thesis under Dr. Stephen Cooper.

My interests include software development, game development, cyber security, artificial intelligence, web development, and more.

CryoCrypt was developed by myself and three others at the 2015 Kansas State University Game Jam, created and prepared for judging in under 48 hours based on a given theme: "Securing the Future." Two teammates used Adobe Photoshop to hand-craft the art for characters and scenery while myself and one other teammate focused on using Unity C# scripts to program the puzzles, algorithms, movement, timers, and more.

This game was awarded Best Art, Best Gameplay, and Best Overall at the First Annual Kansas State University Game Jam.

What have I learned from this?

I learned a lot about programming in Unity during that weekend, because that was my first exposure to Unity. I also learned how to quickly collaborate with a new team in order to build a fully-functioning game. Finally, I improved my presentation skills by presenting to the judges and the general public at the end of the competition.

Is there anything I'd do differently?

Whereas I feel this was a very rewarding and eye-opening experience, I feel like I focused too much on the "competition" aspect and not enough on building comradery with fellow students. I have since learned from this and participated in later competitions with a more open mind.
