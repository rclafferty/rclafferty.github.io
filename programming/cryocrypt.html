CryoCrypt Desktop Video Game (C#)

Note: Designed for Windows computer. Controller is optional.
Should not require admin privileges

CryoCrypt was my first significant step into game development. I worked alongside 3 other team members at the 2015 Kansas State University Game Jam to create this game in a span of 48 hours. As a team, we started with brainstorming storyboard ideas, and then quickly moved into programming, art, and so forth. 2 students used Adobe Photoshop to hand-craft the art for characters and scenery. Another team member and myself both focused on using Unity C# scripts in order to program the puzzles, algorithms, movement, timers, and more. Myself and 1 of the artists also recorded voices for the characters using Audacity. Unfortunately, the voice-overs are not implemented in this build.

This game was awarded Best Art, Best Gameplay, and Best Overall.

What have I learned from this?

I learned a lot about programming in Unity during that weekend, because that was my first exposure to Unity. I also learned how to quickly collaborate with a new team in order to build a fully-functioning game. Finally, I improved my presentation skills by presenting to the judges and the general public at the end of the competition.

Is there anything I'd do differently?

Whereas I feel this was a very rewarding and eye-opening experience, I feel like I focused too much on the "competition" aspect and not enough on building comradery with fellow students. I have since learned from this and participated in later competitions with a more open mind.
