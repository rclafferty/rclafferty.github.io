Basketball 3D

I am a third year master's student at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln studying Computer Science. I also have a Bachelor's of Science in Computer Science from Kansas State University, where I focused my studies on cyber security and video game development. I am currently the president of the UNL Game Development Club and studying educational game development/gamification as my master's thesis under Dr. Stephen Cooper.

My interests include software development, game development, cyber security, artificial intelligence, web development, and more.

Basketball 3D is a small Unity game that emulates a player shooting hoops on a baskeball court. It uses simple shapes (cubes, spheres, etc) to make the scene and currently simple materials to add color. It is a good demo to learn and review 3D physics in Unity.

GitHub Link:
https://github.com/rclafferty/Basketball-3D

Language:
C#

Roles:
UI implementations, Level design, C# scripting, user input, collision detection

Designed for:
Windows

Controls:
Mouse left click to choose options, Arrow Keys or WASD to move, optional controller input

Requires Admin Privileges:
None

Actively Developing:
No

What have I learned from this?

Reviewed 3D physics and controls, Learned about particle effects

Is there anything I'd do differently?

I could expand on the artistic look of the environment more.
